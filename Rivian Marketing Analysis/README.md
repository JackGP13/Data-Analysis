# Rivian EV Pricing Analysis

A solo marketing analytics project from UC Berkeley Haas exploring customer willingness-to-pay (WTP) for a Rivian SUV and the demographic factors that drive it.
## The Question
What are Rivian customers willing to pay, and who is most willing to pay it? The goal was to identify both an implied price point and the demographic profile of Rivian's highest-value customers.

## Data
Stated preference survey of 40 respondents, conducted by a third-party marketing analytics firm. Respondents indicated their willingness to pay for a Rivian SUV alongside demographic information.

## Method
Linear regression modeling WTP as a function of demographic variables. Supplemented with a histogram of WTP distribution across respondents.

## Findings
Younger, politically liberal men showed the highest willingness to pay, suggesting this demographic is Rivian's core high-value segment and the most productive target for marketing spend.
The median stated WTP across respondents was well above $100,000 — notably higher than Rivian's actual starting price of ~$80,000. This is a known limitation of stated preference surveys: (over) stated preference differs from revealed preference. The demographic findings are likely more reliable than the absolute price point.
